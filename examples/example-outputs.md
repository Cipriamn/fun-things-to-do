## Example 1: Weekend Date in Amsterdam

**User prompt:** "Need a cute cheap date idea in Amsterdam"

**Agent response:**

**Reading your vibe:** Romantic but budget-friendly, looking for something with a bit of charm.

**Top picks for you:**

1. **Vondelpark Picnic + Filmmuseum Terrace** — Outdoor · Oud-Zuid
   Grab pastries from a bakery, wander Vondelpark, and end at the EYE Filmmuseum terrace across the water for golden hour views. The ferry is free.
   💡 Go on a weekday late afternoon to avoid crowds

2. **Noordermarkt on Saturday** — Market · Jordaan
   Farmers market with great people-watching, cheap stroopwafels, and organic everything. Walk along the canals after.
   💡 Get there by 10am for the best stalls before it gets packed

3. **NDSM Wharf** — Creative hub · Noord
   Free ferry ride, street art, quirky cafes, and a cool industrial vibe. Pllek has affordable drinks with a beach view.
   💡 Combine with sunset — the light over the water is worth it

4. **Hortus Botanicus** — Garden · Plantage
   Cheap entry, beautiful greenhouses, and way more romantic than you'd expect. The butterfly house is a winner.
   💡 Student discount available, check their site

**Quick picks:**
- 🎯 **Easiest win:** Vondelpark Picnic — zero planning, guaranteed good time
- 💎 **Most unique:** NDSM Wharf — feels like you discovered it yourself
- 💸 **Best on a budget:** Noordermarkt — free to browse, cheap to eat

## Example 2: Chill Evening Solo

**User prompt:** "Something chill to do tonight" (user is in Rotterdam)

**Agent response:**

Quick question — are you looking for something social or more solo/peaceful?

**[User responds: "Solo, just want to unwind"]**

**Reading your vibe:** Quiet evening, no pressure, good atmosphere.

**Top picks for you:**

1. **Witte de Withstraat Window Walk** — Stroll · City Center
   Gallery windows stay lit after closing. Grab a coffee, walk slowly, look at art. Nobody bothers you.
   💡 TENT Rotterdam often has interesting installations visible from outside

2. **Annabel** — Bar/Reading spot · Noord
   Chill bar with a reading-room vibe. Locals go alone, nobody judges. Good wine, soft lighting.
   💡 Grab a seat by the window, bring a book if you want

3. **Fenix Food Factory** — Food hall · Katendrecht
   Get one thing to eat, sit by the water, watch the boats. Closes at 10pm but low-key until then.
   💡 The wine bar in the corner is underrated

**Quick picks:**
- 🎯 **Easiest win:** Witte de Withstraat — just walk, no commitment
- 💎 **Most unique:** Annabel — you'll feel like a local
- 💸 **Best on a budget:** Window walk — literally free

## Example 3: Active But Low Effort

**User prompt:** "I want something active but low effort nearby" (user is in Utrecht)

**Agent response:**

Got it — something that gets you moving without feeling like a workout. Are you thinking outdoors or okay with indoor options?

**[User responds: "Outdoors, nice weather today"]**

**Reading your vibe:** Light activity, outside, no planning or gear needed.

**Top picks for you:**

1. **Canal Walk: Oudegracht Loop** — Walk · City Center
   The classic Utrecht stroll. Walk the lower wharf level, grab a drink halfway, loop back on the other side.
   💡 Start at the Dom Tower end and walk south — less crowded

2. **Griftpark** — Park · East
   Small park, big personality. Good for a slow walk or sitting in the grass. Has a petting zoo if you want animal therapy.
   💡 The hill has a nice view, easy climb

3. **Bike to Amelisweerd** — Cycling · Southeast
   15-min flat ride to a peaceful estate with old trees and trails. Rent a bike if needed, OV-fiets works.
   💡 The tea house there is nice for a reward stop

4. **Wilhelminapark** — Park · East
   Quieter than the center, has a pond, good for a slow loop. Local dog walkers but not crowded.
   💡 Connects to the Botanic Gardens if you want to extend

**Quick picks:**
- 🎯 **Easiest win:** Oudegracht Loop — you're probably already near it
- 💎 **Most unique:** Amelisweerd — feels like an escape
- 💸 **Best on a budget:** Griftpark — free and charming

## Example 4: Non-Touristy Weekend

**User prompt:** "Find something non-touristy to do in Rotterdam this weekend"

**Agent response:**

**Reading your vibe:** You want local, real, not-in-the-guidebook.

**Top picks for you:**

1. **Blijdorp Sunday Market** — Market · North
   Not the main one tourists find. Locals selling random stuff, good for wandering and cheap finds.
   💡 Sunday morning only, gets quiet after noon

2. **Dakpark** — Rooftop park · Delfshaven
   Built on top of a shopping center. Most visitors don't know it exists. Picnic tables, urban garden plots, city views.
   💡 Go at sunset for the best light, bring snacks

3. **Roffa Mon Amour Walking Tour (DIY)** — Neighborhood · Various
   Their zines map out local spots in different neighborhoods. Grab one from a bookshop and explore yourself.
   💡 The West and Katendrecht editions are the best

4. **Het Industriegebouw** — Creative building · City Center
   Open studios, small galleries, independent coffee. Feels like you discovered it.
   💡 Check if there's an event — they sometimes do open nights

**Quick picks:**
- 🎯 **Easiest win:** Dakpark — unexpected and easy to reach
- 💎 **Most unique:** Roffa Mon Amour self-guided — feels like insider knowledge
- 💸 **Best on a budget:** Blijdorp Market — free entry, cheap everything

## Formatting Notes

These examples demonstrate:
- **Interpreted vibe summary** — one line showing the agent understood
- **3-5 curated picks** — not 10, not 20, just the best options
- **Practical tips** — specific, actionable advice for each spot
- **Quick picks section** — easy decision shortcuts
- **Conversational tone** — sounds like a helpful friend, not a search engine
